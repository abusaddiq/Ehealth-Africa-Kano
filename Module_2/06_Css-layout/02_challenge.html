Students take formal courses in professional ethics in a number of professional programs in their respective colleges. Without taking formal courses in their curriculum, students are taught a good amount of the information ethics sprinkled throughout their courses, either in general education or in their major.

Many professions, in order to keep members updated and compliant with their codes, limit the validity of their licenses to specific time periods so members have to renew their licenses. They then tie license renewal to passing of continuing examinations, which helps ensure that members stay knowledgeable in the profession.
